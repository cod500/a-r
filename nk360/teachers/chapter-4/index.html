Chapter Four

Invasion
Lesson Plan

Guiding Question
How did the Haudenosaunee persevere through American invasions?
Download PDF

At a Glance

Student Objectives

Skills

  • Analyze primary source text to determine the effects of American invasions into Haudenosaunee territory.

Content

  • Understand the damage inflicted when George Washington ordered American generals to invade Haudenosaunee territories in response to attacks on American settlements by British, Mohawk, and Seneca warriors.
  • Reflect on the intention of the American military to destroy Haudenosaunee homes and crops.
  • Understand the perseverance of Haudenosaunee communities in rebuilding after the devastation of American attacks.

Teacher Instructions

Activate Prior Knowledge

  1. Ask students: What does perseverance mean to them? What are examples of perseverance? Ask students to consider the Chapter 4 Guiding Question: How did the Haudenosaunee persevere through American invasions?

Question

Student Task

Formative Assessment: Document Analysis

  1. Depending on students’ progress with primary sources in the previous chapters, determine whether direct instruction, guided practice, or independent practice is the best option for students for Documents A, B, C, and D in the document bank. Use the Close-Reading Strategy options for differentiation. After reading each document, students respond to the document analysis questions.
Teacher Tip:  
  • Remind students that the paraphrases in the “Paraphrased Document” tab might be useful support tools for this task. While helpful as support tools, the paraphrases should not be used as supporting evidence.
  • A supported option for this task is a jigsaw. Each member of a small group analyzes a different document and shares their analysis with the rest of the group. Alternatively, assign one document to each small group to analyze together. Then, each group presents their responses to the class.
  1. Students will synthesize their responses in answering the Chapter 4 Guiding Question. Solicit student responses and record them in a visible location for the next chapter.

Students can complete the task in one of two ways:

Start Interactive

To collect answers as a PDF file or a printed document, have students enter responses in the interactive’s boxes. Then, select the “Save as PDF / Print” button.

Open Google Slides

To collect answers in a learning management system, download and assign the Google Slides file within the system.


To collect handwritten answers, download and print the Google Slides file.

Closing

  1. Ask students what stood out to them about the American invasion of Haudenosaunee lands in this chapter. Was there anything that they found surprising or challenging? How does learning about this add to their understanding of the American Revolution?
  2. Instruct students to complete the chapter review using the reference timeline and their responses to the document analysis questions. The chapter review will help students keep track of what they have learned in each chapter to later use in the Summative Assessment, similar to notetaking.
  3. Students will list the challenges faced by the Haudenosaunee in each chapter and the acts of perseverance by Haudenosaunee in the face of those challenges.
