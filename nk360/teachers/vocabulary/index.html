Vocabulary

This page includes definitions of words and phrases found throughout this digital inquiry book.

Chapter 1: Haudenosaunee Lifeways

Agency

Control over one's own choices and actions.

Clan

An extended family group named after animals and birds. Clan is passed down through the mother. People belonging to the same clan are related, regardless of their community location.

Clan Mother

A Clan Mother heads each clan, an extended family within a nation. Clan mothers serve for life and are responsible for preserving their peoples’ way of life. This includes making all the major decisions that affect the clan, naming people, ensuring people are fed, supervising ceremonies, selecting the male leaders, and removing a leader if he does not serve the interests of his people.

Confederacy

An alliance that unites nations to create a peaceful way of making decisions.

The Great Law of Peace

One of the most important events that shaped the Haudenosaunee was the creation of the Gayanesshagowa, the Great Law of Peace. It guides the Haudenosaunee through all aspects of life. A full telling of this history takes several days. It reveals the ways in which the Peacemaker’s teachings emphasized the power of reason, not force, to assure the three principles of the Great Law: righteousness, justice, and health. The Great Law of Peace provides the Haudenosaunee people with instructions on how to treat others, directs them on how to maintain a democratic society, and expresses how reason must prevail to preserve peace.

Longhouse

Home to a family belonging to a specific clan. Arranged with bunk beds on either side of a communal fire which allowed for sleeping and storage for families. There could be many longhouses in a village. In the modern era, longhouses are used as spiritual and political centers.

Chapter 2: War Comes to the Haudenosaunee 

Continental Congress

The first governing body (1774–89) of the thirteen colonies, which later became the United States.

Diplomacy

The practice of building and keeping relationships through discussion, negotiation, and mutual respect.

Diplomat

A person whose job is to represent their country in a foreign country.

Father

(figurative) In Chapter 2, Document A, ‘father’ refers to the British government. In Haudenosaunee culture, a father's role is to provide protection and help settle disputes. The role of fathers is not superior to others, but an important relationship in family and community.

Grand Council

Meeting of Hodiya'neh'son (chiefs) and Gayeñ nehda' Gondi'wehnyoñ ( clan mothers) from each nation of the Haudenosaunee Confederacy to deal with issues affecting all the nations in the Confederacy.

Grievances

Complaints or protests, especially for unfair treatment.

Neutrality

Not supporting either side in a disagreement, competition, or war.

Paraphrased document

A rewording of the text in more modern language. It is not an exact transcription.

Superintendant of Indian Affairs

Representative of the King of England to Native nations.

Chapter 3: Tensions Rise 

Battle of Fort Stanwix (also called the Battle of Oriskany)

British, Mohawk, and Seneca forces fought American and Oneida forces on August 6, 1777.

Cede

To give somebody control of something, especially unwillingly.

Central Fire / Council Fire

(Ganakda'gwennioh) A place where the Haudenosaunee Hodiya'neh'son (chiefs) and Gayeñ nehda' Gondi'wehnyoñ
( clan mothers) meet in a Grand Council .

Liable

Likely to do something.

Stanwix Treaty Line

The Proclamation of 1763 was supposed to stop colonists from settling west of the Appalachian Mountains on Native lands, but it did not. The 1768 Treaty of Fort Stanwix, between the Haudenosaunee and British, was written to prevent conflict over this land. The Haudenosaunee ceded land to the British, and the British redrew the boundary between the Haudenosaunee and colonists.

Chapter 4: Invasion

Adherents

Supporters.

Associates

Allies.

Cosmopolitan

Containing people of different types or from different nations.

Hostile

Military enemy.

Invasion

The act of an army entering another nation by force.

Kiln

A type of oven.

Multinational

Consisting of people from different nations.

Reap

Gain.

Reconstitute

To form an organization or group again in a different way.

Refugee

A person who has been forced to leave their home because there is a war.

Situated

Located.

Terminus of Lake Erie

The eastern end of Lake Erie where it empties into the Niagara River.

Chapter 5: The Practice of Peace

Petitioned

To ask (a person, group, or organization) for something in a formal way.

Reciprocity

A mutual exchange or dependent relationship that maintains harmony and balance through a shared sense of responsibility, care, and respect for what is given and what is received.

Reconciliation

The restoration or process of rebuilding trust, balance, peace, harmony, and connection after a period of conflict.

Sovereignty

An independent government’s right to determine what happens for its own nation.

Treaty

A formal agreement between two or more nations.
