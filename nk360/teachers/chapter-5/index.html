Chapter Five

The Practice of Peace
Lesson Plan

Guiding Question
What were the effects of the American Revolution on the Haudenosaunee?
Download PDF

At a Glance

Student Objectives

Skills

  • Interpret audio/visual primary source evidence to identify and categorize the effects of the Revolution on the Haudenosaunee and their responses to it.

Content

  • Understand that despite the challenges of the American Revolution and the violence that followed, Haudenosaunee people signed a treaty with the United States government to continue to prioritize peace.
  • Understand that, today, the Haudenosaunee Confederacy continues to meet in Grand Council and the Haudenosaunee people continue their lifeways.

Teacher Instructions

Activate Prior Knowledge

  1. As a class, review student examples of perseverance from the Chapter 4 Guiding Question: How did the Haudenosaunee persevere through American invasions?
  2. Introduce the Chapter 5 Guiding Question: What were the effects of the Revolution on the Haudenosaunee?
  3. Ask students to predict, based on what they’ve learned so far in Chapters 1–4, how the Haudenosaunee might be affected by the American Revolution.

  1. Ask students which effects of the Revolution on the Haudenosaunee stood out to them.

Formative Assessment: Video Analysis

  1. Students will read the selection of quotes pulled from the video then determine whether the speaker is describing a challenge for the Haudenosaunee or an act of perseverance by the Haudenosaunee. Students will explain their reasoning in the text box.
Teacher Tip:   Students can listen to the quotes by clicking the audio player.
  1. Students will synthesize their responses in answering the Chapter 5 Guiding Question. Solicit student responses and record them in a visible location for the next chapter.

Students can complete the task in one of two ways:

Closing

  1. Instruct students to complete the chapter review using the reference timeline and their responses to the video analysis questions. The chapter review will help students keep track of what they have learned in each chapter to later use in the Summative Assessment, similar to notetaking.
  2. Students will list the challenges faced by the Haudenosaunee in each chapter and the acts of perseverance by Haudenosaunee in the face of those challenges.
