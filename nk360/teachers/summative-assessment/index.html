Summative Assessment
Lesson Plan

Central Question
In what ways did the Haudenosaunee persevere through and after the American Revolution?
Download PDF

At a Glance

Student Objectives

Skills

  • Construct an argument using evidence from multiple sources about the ways in which the Haudenosaunee persevered through the American Revolution.
  • Use evidence to support an argument in a graphic organizer that will demonstrate the ways in which Haudenosaunee people persevered through the American Revolution.

Content

  • Understand the challenges that Haudenosaunee people faced as a result of the American Revolution, including pressure to fight in a war that was not theirs, facing family members in battle, invasion by the American military, and loss of land.
  • Understand the ways in which the Haudenosaunee persevered throughout the Revolution, including efforts to maintain neutrality, rebuild after invasion, reunite their confederacy after the war, and continue their lifeways.

Teacher Instructions

Activate Prior Knowledge

  1. Return to the digital inquiry book’s central question: In what ways did the Haudenosaunee persevere through and after the American Revolution?
  2. Review the students' responses to chapter Guiding Questions and chapter reviews. Solicit student responses. They should have access to their completed work during this review. The chapter Guiding Questions are:
    • Chapter 1: What was life like for the Haudenosaunee in the early 1770s?
    • Chapter 2: Why did the Haudenosaunee Confederacy want to remain neutral early in the American Revolution?
    • Chapter 3: Why did some of the Haudenosaunee align with different sides in the conflict?
    • Chapter 4: How did the Haudenosaunee persevere through American invasions?
    • Chapter 5: What were the effects of the American Revolution on the Haudenosaunee?
  3. Return to the idea of perseverance. Display students’ list of examples of perseverance from Chapter 1.
  4. After reviewing the list, ask students to consider the examples of Haudenosaunee perseverance they learned about in the digital inquiry book.

Student Task

  1. Direct students to the Summative Task option you have chosen to assign.
  2. There are two differentiated options for students to complete the summative task. The argumentative paragraph is an independent option. The graphic organizer is a supported alternative for students who are not yet comfortable with independent argumentative writing and can also be used to help students prepare to write the argumentative paragraph. An argumentative writing rubric can be adapted to assess either task.
  3. Explain to students that they will use their analyses from each chapter to complete the graphic organizer and/or write the argumentative paragraph. While they might use any evidence from the digital inquiry book, we recommend guiding them toward using primary source evidence either from the documents or video.
Teacher Tip:   Using video as a primary source might take students a bit of practice. Explain to students that the people in the video interviews are also providing primary source evidence, just like the text documents. It might also help to model use of interview quotes. The Chapter 5 Formative Assessment: Video Analysis features quotes from the video.
  1. Model completing the first row of the graphic organizer and/or using the example argument for students or for students who need writing scaffolding.

Summative Task: Graphic Organizer (Supported Option) 

Students will use their formative assessments and chapter reviews to identify challenges faced by the Haudenosaunee and the ways in which Haudenosaunee people persevered in the face of those challenges. Students will support the examples of perseverance by selecting evidence from the document banks and videos from Chapters 1–5. Students will use the provided boxes to enter their responses. 

Students can complete the Graphic Organizer in one of two ways:

Start Interactive

To collect answers as a PDF file or a printed document, have students enter responses in the interactive’s boxes. Then, select the “Save as PDF / Print” button.

Open Google Slides

To collect answers in a learning management system, download and assign the Google Slides file within the system.


To collect handwritten answers, download and print the Google Slides file.

Summative Task: Argumentative Paragraph (Independent Option)

Students will write an argumentative paragraph in response to the central question: “In what ways did the Haudenosaunee persevere through and after the American Revolution?” 

Suggested paragraph format:

  • Students will write a claim and counterclaim using their formative assessments and chapter reviews to identify challenges faced by the Haudenosaunee as a result of the American Revolution and the ways in which Haudenosaunee people persevered in the face of those challenges. 

    • Suggested sentence starter:

    • With counterclaim: Despite/in the face of . . . , Haudenosaunee people persevered through/after the Revolution by . . .
    • For students who are not yet comfortable with counterclaims: The Haudenosaunee persevered through/after the American Revolution by . . .
  • Students will provide supporting evidence with context from the document banks and videos from Chapters 1–5. Their responses to formative assessments and chapter reviews can help them identify which sources to use to support their claim.
  • Students will conclude their argument by explaining the connection between the evidence and the claim.

Students' arguments will likely vary. These sample argument stems with counterclaims, written in the voice of a 6th to 8th grade student, represent just a few of the ideas students may express. If helpful, you might share one or more of the examples to prompt students' thinking about their own conclusions to the central question. 

  • Despite being pressured by both the British and the Americans to join their sides, the Haudenosaunee Confederacy persevered through the Revolution by staying neutral and letting individuals to decide what they wanted to do.
  • Despite being driven from their homes by the American army, Haudenosaunee people persevered through the Revolution by running from the violence and rebuilding at Buffalo Creek.
  • In the face of families fighting each other on opposite sides of the war, Haudenosaunee people persevered after the Revolution by staying united as a confederacy.
  • Despite losing much of their land after the Revolution, Haudenosaunee people persevered after the Revolution by continuing their government and lifeways.

Students can complete the Argumentative Paragraph in one of two ways:

Start Interactive

To collect answers as a PDF file or a printed document, have students enter responses in the interactive’s boxes. Then, select the “Save as PDF / Print” button.

Open Google Slides

To collect answers in a learning management system, download and assign the Google Slides file within the system.


To collect handwritten answers, download and print the Google Slides file.

Closing

  1. Once students have completed their assessment, ask them to consider how their perspective of the American Revolution, Haudenosaunee people, and perseverance has changed since starting the digital inquiry book.
  2. Exit slip: Have students write down additional questions they have about the perspectives of Native peoples during and after the American Revolution. This can be a jumping-off point for the next class discussion or a research project.
