Chapter One

Haudenosaunee Lifeways

Lesson Plan

Guiding Question

What was life like for the Haudenosaunee in the early 1770s?

Introduction Video

The Haudenosaunee are a Confederacy whose homelands are in present-day New York and beyond. The Confederacy is made up of the Mohawk, Oneida, Tuscarora, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca nations. The Haudenosaunee Confederacy is also known as the Six Nations. The nations are united under the Great Law of Peace.

Video Vocabulary

In order of video appearance:

  • Confederacy: an alliance that unites nations to create a peaceful way of making decisions.

  • Longhouse: home to a family belonging to a specific clan. Arranged with bunk beds on either side of a communal fire which allowed for sleeping and storage for families. There could be many longhouses in a village. In the modern era, longhouses are used as spiritual and political centers.
  • The Great Law of Peace: one of the most important events that shaped the Haudenosaunee was the creation of the Gayanesshagowa, the Great Law of Peace. It guides the Haudenosaunee through all aspects of life. A full telling of this history takes several days. It reveals the ways in which the Peacemaker’s teachings emphasized the power of reason, not force, to assure the three principles of the Great Law: righteousness, justice, and health. The Great Law of Peace provides the Haudenosaunee people with instructions on how to treat others, directs them on how to maintain a democratic society, and expresses how reason must prevail to preserve peace.
  • Clan Mother: a Clan Mother heads each clan, an extended family within a nation. Clan mothers serve for life and are responsible for preserving their peoples’ way of life. This includes making all the major decisions that affect the clan, naming people, ensuring people are fed, supervising ceremonies, selecting the male leaders, and removing a leader if he does not serve the interests of his people.
  • Agency: control over one’s own choices and actions.
